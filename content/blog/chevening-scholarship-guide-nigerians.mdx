Chevening is the UK government's flagship international scholarship. Every year, it funds outstanding individuals from around the world to study a one-year Masters programme at any UK university — fully funded.

Nigeria consistently produces one of the largest Chevening cohorts globally. That is the good news. The other side of that coin: Nigerian applicants are also among the most competitive in the world. A strong application is not optional — it is mandatory.

This guide walks you through every stage of the Chevening application process, with specific advice for Nigerian applicants.

---

## What Chevening Covers

Chevening is genuinely fully funded. The award includes:

- **University tuition fees** — paid directly to your university, no cap on fee level
- **Monthly living allowance** — based on London or non-London location
- **Economy class return flights** — from Nigeria to the UK and back
- **UK visa application fee** — reimbursed
- **Arrival allowance** — one-time payment on arrival
- **Homeward departure allowance** — on completion

There is no contribution required from the scholar. You do not need savings, a bank statement, or a sponsor.

---

## Who Is Eligible?

**Nationality:** Nigerian citizen (you can hold dual nationality, but Nigerian citizenship is required)

**Academic:** Undergraduate degree equivalent to a UK 2:1 (Second Class Upper). In Nigeria, this means a minimum of 2:1 from a Nigerian university, though a First Class strengthens your application significantly.

**Work experience:** Minimum **2 years of work experience** by the time you start the scholarship (counted from your undergraduate graduation date, or from when you started working — whichever is later). This is a hard requirement, not a preference. Volunteer work, internships, and NYSC can count if they were full-time and structured.

**Return commitment:** You must commit to returning to Nigeria for at least **two years** after completing the scholarship. Chevening scholars are expected to become leaders and change-makers in their home countries — this is central to the programme's mission.

**You cannot apply if:** You are currently studying in the UK, have a Chevening scholarship currently, are a family member of a British Embassy employee, or have previously been a Chevening scholar.

## Step 1: Choose Your Three Universities

Before you begin the online application, you must identify **three UK university courses** you want to apply to. These are submitted as part of your Chevening application.

**Important rules:**
- All three must be eligible Masters programmes (full-time, one year, starting the October after your application)
- They must be at three different UK universities
- You must apply to all three universities directly (through each university's admissions portal) and have at least one conditional or unconditional offer before Chevening makes its final decision

**How to choose:**
1. Match the programme to your stated career goals in your essays — consistency matters
2. Include at least one top-ranked university (Russell Group) — it demonstrates ambition
3. Consider location (London vs. other cities affects living allowance and cost of living)

**Popular choices for Nigerian Chevening applicants:** University of Sussex (Development Studies), University of Leeds, University of Nottingham, King's College London, UCL, LSE (competitive), University of Birmingham, University of Edinburgh.

## Step 2: The Four Essays

The essays are where most Nigerian applications succeed or fail. Chevening requires four essays, each with a word limit of approximately 500 words:

### Essay 1: Leadership and Influence

**Prompt:** Describe a situation where you have demonstrated leadership and influenced others. What was the outcome?

**What Chevening is looking for:** Real examples of leadership — not a job title, but evidence that you have actually influenced people or outcomes. This could be leading a project at work, organising community action, founding something, or mentoring others.

**Common Nigerian mistakes:** Describing a job title without describing a specific leadership act. "As a manager, I led a team of five" tells them nothing. "I identified that our team's reporting process was creating a 3-day delay in decision-making. I proposed and implemented a new system that reduced reporting time to same-day" is what they want.

### Essay 2: Networking

**Prompt:** Explain how you will use the Chevening network.

**What they want to know:** That you understand Chevening is not just a scholarship — it is a lifelong network of over 50,000 alumni in 160 countries. They want scholars who will actively use and contribute to this network.

**Tip:** Research specific Chevening events, alumni chapters, and programmes before writing this essay. Mention the Nigeria Chevening Alumni Association. Show you've done your homework.

### Essay 3: Studying in the UK

**Prompt:** Why is it important for you to study in the UK specifically?

**Do not write:** "The UK has good universities." Every country does. This essay needs to explain why the UK specifically — for your field. Examples: specific research centres, access to particular industries, unique academic traditions, proximity to European institutions for your research area.

### Essay 4: Career Plan

**Prompt:** Describe your career plan for the next ten years and how your chosen course contributes to achieving it.

**The most important essay.** Chevening funds people who will make an impact. The strongest essays show:
- Where you are now (current role, skills, experience)
- Where you want to be in 10 years (specific, credible, ambitious)
- How your chosen UK Masters closes the gap between where you are and where you want to be
- How Nigeria benefits from your development

**Avoid:** Vague plans ("I want to be a leader in my industry"). Be specific. Name organisations you want to work at. Describe policies you want to influence. Mention the problems you want to solve.

## Step 3: References

You need **two referees** — people who can speak to your professional or academic achievements. At least one should be a professional referee (your current employer or supervisor).

**Good referees:**
- Current or recent line manager / supervisor
- Academic supervisor or lecturer who knows your work well
- Mentor with professional standing

**Poor referees:**
- Friends or family
- Colleagues at the same level as you
- People who only know you socially

Give your referees plenty of time — at least 4–6 weeks. Share your CV, your essays (once drafted), and your career goals with them so their reference speaks to the same themes your essays do.

---

## Step 4: The Interview

Shortlisted applicants are invited to interview in Abuja (at the British High Commission) or Lagos. Interviews typically take place between March and May.

**Format:** Panel interview, approximately 30–45 minutes. Usually 3 interviewers.

**Common questions:**
- "Tell us about yourself and your career so far."
- "Why do you want to study [your chosen course]?"
- "What is the biggest challenge facing Nigeria in [your field], and how do you plan to address it?"
- "How will this scholarship change what you can achieve?"
- "Tell us about a time you led a significant change."
- "How will you use the Chevening network?"

**Preparation:** Practice your answers out loud, with a timer. 2–3 minutes per answer is the target. Know your essays inside out — interviewers read your essays before meeting you.

## The Most Common Reasons Nigerian Applications Fail

1. **Generic essays** — not specific enough about achievements, plans, or Nigeria
2. **Work experience gap** — not meeting the 2-year requirement or not documenting it properly
3. **Mismatched course and career plan** — choosing a course that does not logically connect to stated goals
4. **Applying to only one university** — Chevening requires three choices; applying to fewer limits your options
5. **Late application** — the portal closes sharply at the deadline; technical issues at the last minute are your problem, not Chevening's
6. **Weak references** — referees who describe you generically rather than with specific examples
